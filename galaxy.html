Galaxies

Exploring the vast cosmic systems that shape our universe.

Spiral Galaxies

Spiral galaxies are among the most beautiful structures in the universe. They contain rotating arms filled with stars, gas, and dust. The Milky Way, our home galaxy, is a barred spiral galaxy with billions of stars.

Elliptical Galaxies

Elliptical galaxies have smooth, rounded shapes and contain older stars. They lack the spiral arms found in other galaxies and are often formed through galaxy collisions and mergers over billions of years.

Irregular Galaxies

Irregular galaxies do not follow a specific shape or structure. Their unusual appearance is often caused by gravitational interactions with neighboring galaxies, leading to intense star formation activity.

The Milky Way

The Milky Way is the galaxy that contains our Solar System and is one of the billions of galaxies in the observable universe. It is a barred spiral galaxy with a diameter of about 100,000 light-years and contains hundreds of billions of stars. From Earth, the Milky Way appears as a glowing band of light stretching across the night sky, created by the combined light of distant stars and cosmic dust. Scientists believe a supermassive black hole exists at its center, influencing the movement of stars and other celestial objects throughout the galaxy.
